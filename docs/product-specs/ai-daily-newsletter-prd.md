# AI Daily Newsletter Product Requirements Document

## 1. Objective
Deliver a daily AI newsletter that helps AI practitioners plus product and engineering leaders understand important AI developments in under five minutes, with practical context instead of raw link aggregation.

## 2. Target Audience
Primary:
- Product managers, founders, and engineering leads at startups and SMBs adopting AI.
- Builders who want tactical, high-trust summaries with low noise.

Secondary:
- AI-curious operators who need business and implementation implications instead of research-only depth.

## 3. Value Proposition
Each edition answers three questions quickly:
1. What changed?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What should I do next?

## 4. User Jobs To Be Done
- Understand the most relevant AI updates in less than five minutes.
- Identify one to two actions to apply in product, engineering, or operations.
- Track major ecosystem shifts without information overload.

## 5. Non-Goals
- Full long-form research deep-dives.
- Real-time breaking-news alerting.
- Personalized recommendation ranking in phase one.

## 6. Content Categories and Priority Mix
Target mix per daily issue:
- `Industry News` (30%): launches, announcements, market shifts.
- `Product and Builder Tactics` (30%): practical implementation patterns and workflows.
- `Research to Reality` (20%): key papers translated to practical implications.
- `Tools and Releases` (15%): notable model and tool updates with impact notes.
- `Signals and Watchlist` (5%): trend indicators and what to monitor next.

Editorial rules:
- Prioritize impact and novelty over popularity.
- Cap total items to preserve signal density.

## 7. Email Format and Structure
Required sections:
1. `Subject line`: clear daily hook plus relevance cue.
2. `Top Brief` (one to two items): highest-impact updates plus implication sentence.
3. `What Matters Today` (three to five bullets): concise summaries with links.
4. `Builder Corner` (one tactical takeaway): concrete implementation tip.
5. `Watchlist` (one to three items): emerging developments to monitor.
6. `CTA`: subscribe, share, or reply feedback prompt.

Format constraints:
- Target total length: 400 to 700 words.
- Mobile-first layout with short paragraphs and bullets.
- Every item includes source attribution.

## 8. Quality Standards
Pre-send quality gate:
- `Accuracy`: every factual statement supported by cited sources.
- `Clarity`: each item understandable in no more than 120 words.
- `Actionability`: Builder Corner contains at least one practical recommendation.
- `Signal`: no filler entries; each item has explicit user value.
- `Tone`: concise, neutral, professional.

Rubric and ship thresholds:
- Accuracy: 5/5 required.
- Clarity: at least 4/5.
- Actionability: at least 4/5.
- Signal density: at least 4/5.

## 9. Growth Path
Phase 1 (weeks one to two):
- Launch daily format with manual curation.
- Collect reply feedback and baseline open/click metrics.

Phase 2 (weeks three to six):
- Add referral and share loop in CTA.
- Add lightweight topic-preference polling.

Phase 3 (week seven onward):
- Segment editions by audience intent (builders versus operators).
- Pilot sponsorship once engagement is stable.

## 10. Success Metrics
North-star metric:
- Weekly active readers (opened at least three editions per week).

Core KPIs:
- Open rate at least 35%.
- Click-through rate at least 6%.
- Reply or feedback rate at least 1.5%.
- Unsubscribe rate no more than 0.4% per send.

## 11. Risks and Mitigations
- Noise risk: enforce item cap and signal rubric.
- Credibility risk: enforce citation requirement plus accuracy gate.
- Production bottleneck risk: use section template and category quotas.

## 12. Acceptance Criteria Coverage
- Objective, audience, value proposition, JTBD, and non-goals plus risks are defined.
- Content categories and relative mix percentages are defined.
- Email template structure and word-count constraints are defined.
- Quality rubric and minimum ship thresholds are defined.
- Growth path is documented across three phased milestones.
- Success metrics include one north-star and four measurable KPIs.
